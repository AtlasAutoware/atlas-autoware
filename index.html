Our Mission Meet the Team Project Portfolio

Machine Learning

We use convolutional neural networks (CNNs), reinforcement learning, and other forms of machine learning to develop increasingly capable autonomous robots.

Algorithms

We develop novel algorithms for our robots to make sense of the world around them. From computer vision to graph theory, we use computer science to push our understanding of robotics forward.

Mechanical Engineering

We are building a fully 3D-printable self-driving car which will lower the cost of entry and make this technology more accessible.

Electrical Engineering

We integrate Electrical Engineering (EE) extensively in our robots. From power management systems to complex signaling, EE is a fundamental part of what we do.

Our Mission

Our mission is to build a fully street-legal self-driving car by 2026.

  • 2022

    1/18 Scale Self-Driving Car

    Our 1/18 scale car was our first foray into the world of self-driving vehicles. It uses a standard “hobby chassis” for modularity and easy repair. Our goal for this project was to create a vehicle which could autonomously navigate a defined track, and over the course of many months we achieved this goal. The 1/18 scale car utilizes a spinning LIDAR unit and ESP32 microcontroller for all computation and sensing. Currently, we have built 3 of these cars and we plan to release the CAD files and schematics for it in the near future.

  • 2023

    1/10 Scale “F1/10” Self-Driving Car

    Paragraph: The F1/10 Car is a 1/10 Scale autonomous racecar, a more advanced and refined version of our 1/18 scale robot. We plan to use this car as a testing platform for the algorithms we develop, as well as an entry to the annual F1/10 competition at the International Conference for Robotics and Automation. Furthermore, our 1/10 scale car is a useful way to demonstrate how self-driving cars work to a wider audience in a more approachable way. Currently, the F1/10 car is under construction.

  • 2024

    Atlas Beta

    Beta is an affordable and accessible 3D-Printed automotive robotic platform. The design specifications will be open-source and made freely available to the public. The primary objective of Atlas Beta is to aid middle and high school teachers, as well as extracurricular clubs. The open-source project will provide a guide with assembly instructions as well as a parts list to purchase to to construct this low cost platform in order to teach students about robotics and self-driving vehicles as well as our proprietary algorithms developed specifically for this platform. A kit will also be available for educators to purchase directly from us to simplify this process and to ensure that all the necessary components are included. The kit will contain all the required 3D-printed parts, electronics, sensors, and a comprehensive manual to assist teachers in effectively implementing the curriculum.

  • 2025

    Atlas Alpha

    Alpha is an Autonomous Low Speed Vehicle, street legal on all non interstate roads. Its lightweight chassis and electric AWD powertrain make it the perfect platform to develop autonomous driving algorithms on. Alpha uses an array of depth cameras for perception, fusing the data with GNSS and inertial units to gain full awareness of it's current position in space. Alpha is already in development and will help propel research regarding Autonomous driving in low speed urban environments.

  • 2026

    Atlas Proxima

    Proxima is a full scale Self-Driving Sedan, and will meet Federal regulations for street legal status on every kind of road. Based on the Alpha platform, it will have a lengthened chassis and body, more powerful powertrain, LIDAR perception, and extended range to meet the needs of a Level 4 self-driving vehicle. With its modular body, low cost powertrain and free API, Proxima will set a new standard for open source electric vehicles and their capabilities.
